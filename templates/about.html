Methodology

What the Human Development Index measures

The HDI is a composite statistic developed by the UNDP to argue that people and their capabilities, not economic growth alone, should be the ultimate criteria for assessing a country's development. It combines three dimensions into a single 0–1 score.

01 Health
Life expectancy at birth LEI
02 Education
Mean + expected years of schooling EI
03 Standard of living
GNI per capita, PPP II

The formula

LEI = (Life Expectancy − 20) / (85 − 20) MYSI = Mean Years of Schooling / 15 EYSI = Expected Years of Schooling / 18 EI = (MYSI + EYSI) / 2 II = (ln(GNI per capita) − ln(100)) / (ln(75000) − ln(100)) HDI = ( LEI × EI × II ) ^ (1/3) ← geometric mean

Development tiers

TierHDI rangeTypical profile
Very High ≥ 0.800 Long lifespans, near-universal secondary/tertiary education, high income
High 0.700 – 0.799 Strong outcomes with some gaps versus advanced economies
Medium 0.550 – 0.699 Uneven progress across health, education, or income
Low < 0.550 Structural constraints across most development dimensions

How this app's models work

A synthetic dataset of 660 country-profiles was generated by sampling three development archetypes (advanced, emerging, and developing economies) and computing each profile's true HDI using the formula above, then adding small measurement noise. Two models are trained on this data:

  • RandomForestClassifier — predicts the discrete tier (Very High / High / Medium / Low) from the four raw indicators.
  • RandomForestRegressor — predicts the continuous HDI score (0–1) from the same indicators.

See the model dashboard for accuracy, R², confusion matrix, and feature-importance charts.
